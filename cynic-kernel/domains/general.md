# General Domain — Axiom Evaluation Criteria

Evaluate the CLAIM, ARGUMENT, or ARTIFACT described — not the quality of its prose. A valid insight poorly expressed is still valid. An eloquent argument with no substance is still empty.

Judge the SUBSTANCE: is the reasoning sound? Are the claims supported? Would this survive hostile questioning?

## FIDELITY
Is this faithful to observable reality? Are claims consistent with verifiable evidence?
- HIGH: Specific evidence cited. Claims match the data. Internal consistency — no contradictions. Sources traceable.
- MEDIUM: Directionally correct but imprecise. Some claims unsupported. Minor inconsistencies.
- LOW: Contradicts known facts. Claims without evidence. Internal contradictions. Wishful thinking presented as analysis.

## PHI
Is this well-structured? Does the strength of claims match the strength of evidence? Are the parts proportional to the whole?
- HIGH: Clear logical structure. Claim magnitude matches evidence magnitude. No section dominates disproportionately. Balanced treatment.
- MEDIUM: Readable structure with minor imbalances. Some claims outrun their evidence.
- LOW: Disorganized. Extraordinary claims from ordinary evidence. Key sections missing or bloated.

## VERIFY
Does this SURVIVE scrutiny? When tested against the strongest counterargument, does it hold?
- HIGH: Falsifiable claims with stated conditions. Counterarguments addressed. Reasoning survives hostile questioning. Limitations acknowledged.
- MEDIUM: Falsifiable in principle but counterarguments not addressed. Conclusions plausible but untested against alternatives.
- LOW: Unfalsifiable assertions. No counterargument considered. Circular reasoning. Conclusions assumed rather than derived.

## CULTURE
Does this honor established knowledge and conventions in its domain? Does it build on what came before?
- HIGH: Builds on existing body of knowledge. Cites precedent. Deviations from convention are justified with concrete reasons.
- MEDIUM: Aware of conventions but doesn't deeply engage with prior work. Some reinvention without acknowledgment.
- LOW: Ignores established knowledge. Reinvents poorly what already exists. No awareness of prior art.

## BURN
Is this the simplest sufficient expression? Could the same point be made with less?
- HIGH: Every element serves the argument. No redundancy. Maximum insight per unit of complexity. Parsimonious.
- MEDIUM: Mostly efficient with some unnecessary elaboration. Core message clear despite excess.
- LOW: Bloated. Key point buried in filler. Could be cut by half without losing substance. Complexity without explanatory power.

## SOVEREIGNTY
Does this preserve independent judgment? Is the reasoning self-standing or dependent on authority?
- HIGH: Argument stands on its own logic and evidence. Multiple perspectives considered. Acknowledges uncertainty. Not captive to a single source or narrative.
- MEDIUM: Relies on some authority but with reasoning provided. Mostly independent but some unexamined assumptions.
- LOW: Argument from authority alone. Single-source dependency. No independent reasoning. Echoes a narrative without examination.
