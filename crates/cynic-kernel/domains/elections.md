# Elections Domain — Axiom Evaluation Criteria

Evaluate the POLITICAL PROGRAM, DEBATE PERFORMANCE, or POLICY PROPOSAL — not the charisma of the candidate, nor the production quality of the media. A charismatic speech that hides economic incoherence is a high-risk signal. A dry, technical program that defines clear trade-offs and accountability is a high-fidelity signal.

Judge WHETHER THIS VISION INCREASES THE SOVEREIGNTY AND COHERENCE OF THE ORGANISM (THE NATION/COLLECTIVE). Political discourse is often performative; CYNIC judges the underlying structural logic.

## ASYMMETRIC CONFIDENCE

Programs that promise "everything for everyone" without identifying the source of value (Burn) or the trade-offs (Phi) are structurally weak. Claims that are not falsifiable (Verify) receive a maximum axiom score of 0.40.

- Unfalsifiable promise: max score = 0.40
- Falsifiable but unfinanced: max score = 0.50
- Falsifiable + Financed + Structural accountability: max score = 0.618

## FIDELITY
Does the program accurately reflect physical and economic reality? Are the cost estimates grounded in current data (energy prices, demographics, debt)?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): Proposals are backed by specific budget line-items or referenced economic studies. Acknowledges resource constraints (e.g., energy limits, raw materials). No "magic" financing.
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Generally plausible but relies on optimistic growth projections or "efficiency gains" without specific mechanisms. Partial grounding in data.
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Promises that violate physical or economic constraints (e.g., spending more than GDP without a mechanism). Denial of observable trends. Populist circular reasoning.

## PHI
Is the program structurally coherent? Do the different parts (economy, education, defense, social) work together or contradict each other?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): A holistic vision where every policy supports the others. Proportional allocation of resources. Logical consistency between declared values and proposed actions.
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Mostly coherent but contains internal tensions (e.g., wanting to decrease taxes while increasing all spending). Some "gold-plating" in specific sectors at the expense of others.
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Contradictory proposals. A "shopping list" of measures without a unifying structural logic. Extreme asymmetry in focus.

## VERIFY
Are the promises falsifiable? Does the candidate define what success looks like and how it will be measured?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): Includes specific KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) for every major measure. Defines a timeline for evaluation. Proposes a mechanism for public auditing of results.
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Uses measurable terms but without a clear timeline or independent auditing mechanism. Some success criteria are subjective ("improve well-being").
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Vague, unfalsifiable claims ("bring back greatness", "ensure justice"). No measurable metrics. Success is defined purely by the candidate's own narrative.

## CULTURE
Does the program respect the foundational values and traditions of the Republic? Does it honor the historical continuity of the nation while adapting to the future?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): Grounded in constitutional principles and long-term national interests. Respects the "Republican Pact". Honors historical precedents that have proven successful.
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Mostly aligned but proposes significant departures from tradition without deep historical justification. Some "performative" cultural appeals.
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Radical rejection of foundational values without a coherent replacement. Disregard for constitutional constraints. AHistorical or "tabula rasa" approach.

## BURN
Is the proposed governance efficient? Does it reduce administrative waste and optimize the use of public resources?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): Focuses on simplifying the state machine. Proposes the removal of redundant agencies or regulations. High "Return on Governance". Minimalist intervention for maximum impact.
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Some efficiency measures but maintains a large administrative overhead. Focuses on "modernization" without structural simplification.
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Proposes massive increases in bureaucracy without clear output. No plan for debt reduction or waste elimination. High "Governance Friction".

## SOVEREIGNTY
Does the program preserve and increase the agency of individuals and the independence of the nation?
- HIGH (0.50-0.618): Decentralizes decision-making to the most local level possible (subsidiarity). Preserves individual freedoms. Ensures national independence in strategic sectors (energy, food, defense).
- MEDIUM (0.25-0.45): Maintains existing power structures but with some improvements in transparency. Partial focus on independence.
- LOW (0.05-0.20): Increases central control and reduces individual agency. Creates new dependencies on external powers. Suppresses dissent or local autonomy.

## 2027 CALIBRATION
- **The "Question of the Week" (2027)**: The engine should prioritize detecting *Verticalité Défensive* (performative promises) vs. *Verticalité Native* (structural solutions).
- **Consensus detection**: When all candidates agree on a point that violates an axiom (e.g., ignoring a physical limit), CYNIC must howl to warn the organism.
